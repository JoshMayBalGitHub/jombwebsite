Introduction

The Renaissance was a fervent period of European cultural, artistic, political and economic “rebirth” following the Middle Ages. Generally described as taking place from the 14th century to the 17th century, the Renaissance promoted the rediscovery of classical philosophy, literature and art.


Florence, Italy

Rise of The Renaissance

The most important factor is the location of Italy. Italy is located between Western Asia and West Europe. Because of this the city-states in Italy had the opportunity to trade with countries in Western Asia and Western Europe. It also helped Italy to receive different philosophies from East and West.

Italy is also the origin of Ancient Rome and all of its greatness. Because of this, Italians also consider Rome as the origin of their blood and language it is more relevant to them than any other European country. Because of this belief, they have more desire to return to classical Roman civilization.

The Italian universities also played an important role, as they promoted and emphasized the classical culture, theological knowledge and the philosophies of Greek and Roman Civilization. By having free studying, people's view of life became practical and became more free to cultivate their abilities and preferences.

They also had 2 Beliefs:
1. People must be free to cultivate their abilities and prefrence.
2. People must desire happiness and pleasure in the present.

Also, people of royal lineage that are diligent in art and learning such as the Medici Family are also important in the Renaissance including the lead person Lorenzo da Medici, who spread the Renaissance by building a public library that became a learning center for painters and sculptors.

Humanism

According to the International Humanist and Ethical Union, Humanism's definition is as a democratic or ethical status, which reinforces the view that people have the rights and responsibilities to define their own lives as valued.

In literary criticism, Humanism recognizes a person's ability to think and decide his own destiny. According to the belief of humanists, a person is the measure of all things so it is important to give him the freedom to express his feelings and freedom of decision.

The theory of Humanism comes from the humanistic principles and beliefs in literary tradition in Europe during the renaissance where attention is focused on valuing people.

The Perspective on Humanism:
A person's attitude and feelings are the main subject.
They are supposed to appreciate the freedom of the mind, the unique talents, abilities and human nature.
The purpose of literature about the theory of Humanism is to show that the human species is the center of the world where attention is focused on the strengths and good qualities of a person.

Contributions of People to the Renaissance

Literature Arts Science

Literature

Francesco Petrarch

Petrarch's rediscovery of Cicero's letters is often credited with initiating the 14th-century Italian Renaissance and the founding of Renaissance humanism.

Giovanni Boccaccio

A Tuscan-Italian poet and scholar, best remembered as the author of the earthy tales in the Decameron.

William Shakespeare

was an English playwright, poet and actor. He is widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and the world's pre-eminent dramatist. He is often called England's national poet and the "Bard of Avon" (or simply "the Bard").

Desiderius Erasmus

was a Dutch philosopher and Catholic theologian who is considered one of the greatest scholars of the northern Renaissance. As a Catholic priest, he was an important figure in classical scholarship who wrote in a pure Latin style.

Francois Rabelais

was a French Renaissance writer, physician, Renaissance humanist, monk and Greek scholar. He is primarily known as a writer of satire, of the grotesque, and of bawdy jokes and songs.

Miguel de Cervantes

was an Early Modern Spanish writer widely regarded as the greatest writer in the Spanish language and one of the world's pre- eminent novelists. He is best known for his novel Don Quixote, a work often cited as both the first modern novel and "the first great novel of world literature".

Niccolo Machiavelli

was an Italian diplomat, author, philosopher and historian who lived during the Renaissance. He is best known for his political treatise The Prince, he has often been called the father of modern political philosophy and political science.

Arts

Leonardo da Vinci

was an Italian polymath of the High Renaissance who was active as a painter, draughtsman, engineer, scientist, theorist, sculptor, and architect.

Michaelangelo Buonarottii

was an Italian sculptor, painter, architect, and poet of the High Renaissance. Born in the Republic of Florence, his work was inspired by models from classical antiquity and had a lasting influence on Western art.

Raphael Santi

was an Italian painter and architect of the High Renaissance. His work is admired for its clarity of form, ease of composition, and visual achievement of the Neoplatonic ideal of human grandeur.

Science

Issac Newton

was an English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, alchemist, theologian, and author who was described in his time as a "natural philosopher". He was a key figure in the philosophical revolution of the Enlightenment.

Galileo di Vincenzo Bonaiuti de' Galilei

was an Italian astronomer, physicist and engineer, sometimes described as a polymath. Commonly referred to as Galileo, he was born in the city of Pisa, then part of the Duchy of Florence.

Nicolaus Copernicus

was a Renaissance polymath, active as a mathematician, astronomer, and Catholic canon, who formulated a model of the universe that placed the Sun rather than Earth at its center.
