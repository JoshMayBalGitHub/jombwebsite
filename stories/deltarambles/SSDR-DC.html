Well, in short, as said in the webpage itself; > Articles/Stories older than a year that has been deemed important enough to archive, but not enough to be front and center at the normal navigator! I have been thinking about figuring out what to do with stuff that shouldn't be **overtaking** space of my more **developed** work. Such as my older commentaries, older spinoffs and older drafts that no longer fit the current vision of the current story. Of course, the *vision* changes over time, so it isn't necessarily set in stone what is to be thrown to the archive and what stays as a *'main'* thing to look at. After all, you're looking at a webnovel, things can be **changed** because of a whim from the website owner, and luckily, *I am* the website owner... Well, **supposedly** anyway. Both websites (normal and BS-type) are stored in other people's servers anyway, but the point is that I also control the design and look. Looking from it from a reader's perspective, I couldn't put my more **personal** yet unrelated works into someone's website just because its vaguely connected to the story. So when I finish **my** story, I am open to hosting it somewhere **more** popular, but until that happens, it's going to **stay** here. So, in my **own** website, I can put everything and don't have to worry **much** about what people think because unless they email or post at my GitLab issues, it doesn't exist to me. So, an audience can't exist because I can't observe what they'd say or how'd they interact with me. I.. don't know *why* I got there, but the point of the **rambling** is that I have my own website, and with that, I have to **also** think about the future and how a viewer might navigate it. It's been hard to keep up with my own lore, as I've been captivated by other media nowadays as mentioned. But, I don't just.. suddenly run out of ideas, y'know. It's just.. well, when you have limited time to write, a lot of it, if you're really strapped for time, and want to feel satisfied, AI's there for you. ### Oooh, AI, spooky~! AI, **AI,** ***AI***! Well, LLM type. The Generative one. The one people most use. I.. wonder to myself if this has been stifling or freeing, as taking into account both writer wisdom and new technology advancements. I can see where it'd be bad to stop human ingenuity just by having AI do the **'hard work'** of well, writing. The sentence structure, word choices, and general voice of the writer all gets generated by something that as far as we know, uses algorithms to compile everyone else's writing. We're far more complex than algorithms. Especially in Creative Fiction Writing. But, there tends to be a point where yes, it's blatantly AI, but it's readable enough with prompts and contexts that people are satisfied with that instead of making the big (admittedly time-consuming) effort to do it all on their own. I think I've talked about this before, but well, it's still happening. So, until it doesn't affect my writing, I'll keep mentioning it. Back to the point, that aspect of effort conflicts with the general societal norms of focused time and effort on school or work causing these specific type of hobbies to be more daunting to the average person. It also encourages more self-conscious and unskilled (in terms of being compared to masters of writing) writers to not try it and just let AI do it, since 'they weren't going to do it anyway'. How this connects to me is a lot of my ideas get shoved into AI, and I have to either take back my idea and write it in a non-AI influenced way which is just normal writing with extra baggage or they're lost to the AI chat history, never appearing unless thought of again. I find this is the cycle that I've been placed in, as part of the current turbulent times of my life. I wish I can sit down, forego responsibilities and forge my world once more, but.. that's only a wish that can never be fulfilled in it's literal sense. And, it's more to do with my lifestyle and time-blindness interconnecting with AI's prevalence that further cements the stagnation of DeltaRambles and generally, original creation. ### What to do?
